# FitForge Knowledge Base

## General Rules
1. FitForge is an AI-powered fitness platform connecting users with professional trainers.
2. The AI Assistant (FitForge AI) is powered by Google Gemini and is available 24/7 to answer fitness questions.

## Diet Guidelines
3. For weight loss, a caloric deficit of 300-500 calories per day is recommended.
4. Protein intake should be around 1.6g to 2.2g per kilogram of body weight for muscle gain.
5. Hydration is key: Drink at least 3-4 liters of water daily, especially on workout days.
6. A standard healthy breakfast could include oats, eggs, and a piece of fruit. Puttu (a traditional Kerala dish) contains about 250-300 calories per serving depending on the coconut ratio.
7. Meal timing matters: consume a protein-rich meal within 30-60 minutes after a workout for optimal muscle recovery.
8. Avoid ultra-processed foods, excessive sugar, and trans fats. Focus on whole foods: vegetables, lean proteins, healthy fats, and complex carbohydrates.

## Workout Guidelines
9. Progressive overload is the gradual increase of stress placed upon the body during exercise training. It is the most important principle for muscle growth.
10. A beginner should train 3 to 4 days a week, focusing on full-body workouts or upper/lower splits.
11. Rest days are crucial. Muscles grow during rest, not during the workout. Aim for 7-9 hours of sleep.
12. Best exercises for weight loss include a mix of strength training (like squats, deadlifts) and cardio (HIIT, running).
13. Compound movements like squats, deadlifts, bench press, and overhead press give the most muscle activation and calorie burn per exercise.
14. HIIT (High-Intensity Interval Training): alternating between intense bursts and recovery periods. Example: 30 seconds sprint, 30 seconds walk, repeated 10 times.

## Troubleshooting
15. If you experience sharp joint pain during an exercise, stop immediately and consult a doctor or your FitForge trainer. Muscle soreness (DOMS) is normal 24-48 hours after a workout.
16. Plateau in weight loss: if stuck for more than 2 weeks, consider adjusting calorie intake, changing workout type, or doing a diet break for 1-2 weeks.

## Fitness & Nutrition Science
36. BMR (Basal Metabolic Rate): the calories your body burns at rest. FitForge uses the Mifflin-St Jeor formula: Men: (10 x weight kg) + (6.25 x height cm) - (5 x age) + 5. Women: (10 x weight kg) + (6.25 x height cm) - (5 x age) - 161.
37. TDEE (Total Daily Energy Expenditure) = BMR x Activity Multiplier. Sedentary=1.2, Light=1.375, Moderate=1.55, Very Active=1.725.
38. Macronutrients: Protein = 4 cal/gram, Carbohydrates = 4 cal/gram, Fat = 9 cal/gram.
39. Creatine monohydrate is one of the most researched supplements for strength and muscle gain. Standard dose: 3-5g per day.
40. Intermittent fasting (16:8 method) can be effective for fat loss but is not ideal for everyone, especially those focused on muscle building who need frequent protein intake.
41. US Navy Body Fat Formula (Men): 495 / (1.0324 - 0.19077 * log10(waist - neck) + 0.15456 * log10(height)) - 450.
42. Healthy body fat ranges: Men: 10-20% (fit), Women: 18-28% (fit). Above 25% for men or 35% for women is generally considered overweight.
